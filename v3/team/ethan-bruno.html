About Ethan

Ethan Bruno's path to physical therapy is unlike anyone else's on the Resolve Physical Therapy team, and that is exactly what makes him such an effective clinician. Before earning his Doctorate of Physical Therapy from Oregon State University, Ethan spent years as a kindergarten teacher, a role that demanded extraordinary patience, creativity, and the ability to communicate complex concepts in ways that anyone can understand. Those skills transfer directly into his work as a physical therapist, where patient education is not just a component of treatment but a cornerstone of his entire approach.

Originally from Washington, D.C., Ethan has lived in Portland and Flagstaff before settling in Bend, Oregon. Each of those cities contributed to his identity as a practitioner. The urban pace and diversity of Washington, D.C. gave him perspective on a wide range of patient backgrounds and needs. Portland's culture of wellness and movement deepened his appreciation for holistic health. And Flagstaff's outdoor recreation community, with its altitude, trails, and active lifestyle, planted the seed for the kind of physical therapy practice he wanted to build. Bend, with its world-class outdoor access and health-conscious community, is where all of those influences came together.

Patient Education and Communication

What sets Ethan apart is his ability to make the complicated feel simple. Many patients walk into a physical therapy clinic overwhelmed by their diagnosis, confused about their body, and uncertain about what recovery actually looks like. Ethan meets that uncertainty with clear, jargon-free explanations that help patients understand exactly what is happening in their body, why it hurts, and what they are going to do about it. His background in teaching gives him an intuitive sense for how to break down complex movement concepts, anatomy, and exercise mechanics into language that resonates with each individual patient, regardless of their prior medical knowledge.

This educational approach is not about lecturing. Ethan makes learning interactive and engaging. He uses real-time demonstration, hands-on cueing, and visual analogies to help patients develop body awareness and motor control. When a patient understands the reasoning behind each exercise, they perform it better, they are more consistent with their home program, and they take ownership of their recovery. Ethan's patients often describe leaving their sessions feeling not just physically better but genuinely smarter about their own bodies.

Musculoskeletal Care

Ethan's clinical expertise centers on musculoskeletal care, the broad category of conditions affecting muscles, bones, joints, tendons, and ligaments. This includes everything from acute injuries like ankle sprains and muscle strains to chronic conditions like persistent low back pain, rotator cuff tendinopathy, and knee osteoarthritis. His approach to musculoskeletal treatment combines thorough movement assessments with personalized exercise programs that are designed around functional recovery, meaning the exercises he prescribes are directly relevant to the activities and demands of each patient's daily life.

Rather than relying on generic exercise handouts or one-size-fits-all protocols, Ethan builds each treatment plan from the ground up based on what he observes in the examination room. He watches how patients move, identifies the specific patterns that are contributing to their pain or limitation, and designs targeted interventions to address those findings. This might include progressive strengthening exercises to address a specific weakness, mobility work to restore a restricted joint, neuromuscular re-education to improve coordination and control, or manual therapy to reduce pain and facilitate movement. Every component of the plan has a clear purpose, and Ethan makes sure his patients understand what that purpose is.

Functional Recovery and Movement Education

Ethan believes deeply in the concept of functional recovery. This means he does not just measure progress in degrees of range of motion or pounds of resistance. He measures it in the real-world activities his patients can perform. Can you bend down to pick up your grandchild without pain? Can you hike Pilot Butte without your knee giving out? Can you sit at your desk all day and drive home without your back seizing up? These are the benchmarks that matter to Ethan, because they are the benchmarks that matter to his patients.

His movement education philosophy extends to injury prevention as well. Ethan takes time to teach patients how their movement habits, posture, and daily routines may be contributing to their condition. He provides practical strategies for modifying workstation ergonomics, adjusting exercise technique, and incorporating targeted mobility and strengthening work into daily routines. The goal is not just to get a patient better but to give them the tools and knowledge to stay better, reducing the likelihood of recurrence and building long-term resilience.

Post-Operative Rehabilitation

Post-operative rehabilitation is another key component of Ethan's practice. Whether a patient is recovering from a total hip replacement, ACL reconstruction, meniscus repair, or shoulder surgery, Ethan follows evidence-based protocols while maintaining the flexibility to adjust the plan based on how each individual patient responds. He communicates regularly with surgical teams to ensure alignment on goals and timelines, and he uses his teaching skills to keep patients informed and motivated throughout what can be a long and sometimes challenging recovery process.

Outside the Clinic

When he is not in the clinic, Ethan takes full advantage of everything Bend, Oregon has to offer. Winter months find him on the slopes at Mt. Bachelor, where his love of skiing feeds the same drive for movement and physical challenge that drew him to physical therapy. When the snow melts, he trades the skis for a bike, exploring Bend's extensive mountain biking and road cycling networks. Soccer and ultimate frisbee round out his athletic pursuits, providing the team-based competition and camaraderie that have been a part of his life since his days on the East Coast.

Ethan also has a creative side that might surprise those who know him only from the clinic. He is a dedicated ceramicist, spending time at the wheel crafting pottery that demands the same patience, focus, and hand precision that characterize his manual therapy techniques. There is a meditative quality to ceramics that Ethan values, a chance to slow down, work with his hands, and create something tangible. It is a balance to the fast-paced, problem-solving nature of clinical work, and it keeps his hands sharp and his mind refreshed.
