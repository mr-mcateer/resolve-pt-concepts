About Jenny

Jenny McAteer founded Resolve Physical Therapy in 2018 with a singular vision: to create a clinic where every patient receives the undivided, one-on-one attention they deserve. After earning her Doctorate of Physical Therapy from Idaho State University in Pocatello, Idaho, and practicing as a licensed physical therapist since 2010, Jenny saw firsthand what happens when clinics prioritize volume over quality. Patients get shuffled between therapists, spend time with aides instead of their DPT, and leave feeling like just another appointment on the schedule. She knew there had to be a better way.

That conviction led her to open Resolve Physical Therapy on the westside of Bend, Oregon, where the model is simple and non-negotiable: your therapist treats you from start to finish, every single visit. No assistants, no handoffs, no shortcuts. When you walk through the door at Resolve, you work directly with your Doctor of Physical Therapy for the entire session. This approach fosters a deeper understanding of each patient's condition, builds trust, and produces better outcomes because your therapist knows your body, your history, and your goals intimately.

Running Rehabilitation and DorsaVi Technology

Jenny has developed a particular expertise in running rehabilitation, driven by both professional passion and personal experience as an active member of the Central Oregon outdoor community. She utilizes DorsaVi movement sensor technology and AxIT force plates to conduct detailed biomechanical gait analyses that go far beyond what the eye can see. These wireless sensors attach to a runner's body and capture precise data on movement patterns, muscle activation timing, joint angles, and loading forces in real time. The result is an objective, data-driven picture of how a runner moves, where mechanical inefficiencies exist, and what specific interventions will address the root cause of injury rather than simply managing symptoms.

This technology-forward approach to running rehab is especially valuable for athletes dealing with recurring injuries. Many runners experience the same frustrating cycle: pain develops, they rest, the pain fades, they return to running, and the pain comes back. Jenny breaks this cycle by identifying the underlying biomechanical contributors with DorsaVi sensor data and AxIT force plate analysis and building targeted treatment plans that address the true source of the problem. Whether the issue stems from hip weakness, poor cadence, overstride patterns, or ankle mobility restrictions, the data tells the story and the treatment follows the evidence.

Vestibular Therapy

Beyond running, Jenny is a skilled vestibular therapist treating patients with dizziness, vertigo, and balance disorders. Vestibular conditions can be deeply disruptive to daily life, and many patients arrive at Resolve after months or even years of unresolved symptoms. Jenny's thorough assessment process identifies whether the issue originates in the inner ear, the central nervous system, or from cervicogenic sources, and she implements targeted repositioning maneuvers, habituation exercises, and balance retraining protocols tailored to each patient's specific diagnosis. Her patients consistently report significant improvements in their symptoms and a return to the activities they love.

Whiplash and Motor Vehicle Injury Care

Jenny also brings extensive experience treating whiplash and motor vehicle accident injuries. These cases often involve complex presentations with overlapping symptoms including neck pain, headaches, dizziness, jaw dysfunction, and radiating arm pain. Jenny's comprehensive approach addresses each component of the injury systematically, combining manual therapy techniques with progressive strengthening and motor control exercises. She understands the unique challenges that come with auto injury cases, including navigating insurance requirements and documenting functional progress, and she works to make the process as smooth and supportive as possible for her patients.

Post-Operative Rehabilitation

Post-surgical rehabilitation is another cornerstone of Jenny's practice. Whether a patient is recovering from a total knee replacement, ACL reconstruction, rotator cuff repair, or spinal surgery, Jenny develops individualized rehab protocols that align with the surgeon's guidelines while pushing toward the best possible functional outcome. Her methodical, evidence-based progression ensures patients regain strength, mobility, and confidence at a pace that is both safe and challenging. The one-on-one treatment model is particularly critical in post-operative care, where subtle changes in swelling, range of motion, or pain patterns need to be monitored closely and responded to immediately.
