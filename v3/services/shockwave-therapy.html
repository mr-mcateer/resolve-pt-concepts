What Is Focused Shockwave Therapy?

Focused shockwave therapy, also known as extracorporeal shockwave therapy (ESWT), is a non-invasive medical treatment that uses high-energy acoustic waves to stimulate healing in damaged tissues. Originally developed in the 1980s for breaking up kidney stones without surgery, shockwave technology has since been adapted for musculoskeletal applications and is now widely used in physical therapy clinics to treat chronic tendon injuries, calcifications, and soft tissue pain conditions that have not responded to conventional treatments.

At Resolve Physical Therapy in Bend, Oregon, we offer focused shockwave therapy as part of our comprehensive approach to musculoskeletal care. Unlike radial shockwave devices, which disperse energy broadly across the skin surface, our focused shockwave unit delivers concentrated acoustic energy precisely to the target tissue at a specific depth. This allows for more effective treatment of deeper structures such as tendons, entheses, and calcified deposits while minimizing discomfort to surrounding tissues.

Why Focused Shockwave Is the Gold Standard

Not all shockwave therapy is the same. Radial shockwave devices — the more common type found in many clinics — generate pressure waves that disperse from the skin surface inward, losing energy as they penetrate. Their effective treatment depth is limited to approximately 3 to 4 centimeters. Focused shockwave therapy, the technology we use at Resolve PT, concentrates acoustic energy through a precisely engineered lens to reach depths of up to 12 centimeters with consistent intensity. This means we can effectively treat deep structures like the Achilles tendon insertion, calcified rotator cuff deposits, and proximal hamstring attachments that radial devices simply cannot reach. The result is more precise targeting, fewer sessions needed, and better outcomes for complex conditions.

Shockwave therapy is particularly valuable for patients who have been dealing with chronic tendon pain for months or years without resolution. If you have tried rest, ice, stretching, bracing, cortisone injections, or standard physical therapy without lasting improvement, focused shockwave therapy may provide the breakthrough your body needs to begin healing. Our first session is completely free so you can experience the treatment firsthand and determine whether it is right for your condition.

How Shockwave Therapy Works

Focused shockwave therapy works by delivering controlled acoustic pulses to injured tissue, triggering a cascade of biological responses that promote healing and tissue regeneration. The mechanism of action involves several interconnected processes:

Mechanical stimulation: The acoustic waves create rapid pressure changes in the tissue, which mechanically stimulate cells and break up calcified deposits, scar tissue adhesions, and fibrotic tissue that can impede healing. This mechanical disruption restarts the body's natural healing cascade in tissues that have become stagnant or stuck in a chronic inflammatory state.

Neovascularization: Shockwave therapy stimulates the formation of new blood vessels in the treated area. This increased blood supply delivers more oxygen, nutrients, and growth factors to the damaged tissue, creating an environment that supports healing and tissue regeneration. For chronic tendon conditions, where poor blood supply is often a contributing factor to non-healing, this neovascularization effect is particularly significant.

Cellular response: The acoustic energy stimulates the release of growth factors and other signaling molecules that promote tissue repair. Cells in the treated area respond by increasing their metabolic activity, producing new collagen, and reorganizing damaged tissue structures. Research has shown that shockwave therapy increases the expression of vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF), bone morphogenetic protein (BMP), and other proteins essential for tissue healing.

Pain modulation: Shockwave therapy has been shown to reduce pain through multiple mechanisms, including the destruction of pain-transmitting nerve fibers (substance P depletion), the stimulation of endorphin release, and the reduction of inflammatory mediators in the treated tissue. Many patients report a significant reduction in pain within the first few sessions of treatment.

Conditions Treated with Shockwave Therapy

Focused shockwave therapy is effective for a range of musculoskeletal conditions, particularly chronic tendon disorders that have not responded to conservative treatment. At Resolve Physical Therapy, we commonly use shockwave therapy for the following conditions:

  • Plantar fasciitis — Chronic heel pain caused by inflammation and degeneration of the plantar fascia. Shockwave therapy is one of the most well-researched and effective treatments for plantar fasciitis that has persisted beyond six months. Studies show success rates of 70 to 80 percent in patients who had failed other conservative treatments.
  • Achilles tendinopathy — Chronic pain and thickening of the Achilles tendon, common in runners, hikers, and active individuals in the Bend, Oregon area. Shockwave therapy promotes collagen remodeling and neovascularization in the damaged tendon, helping it heal and regain strength.
  • Rotator cuff injuries — Calcific tendinitis and chronic tendinopathy of the rotator cuff tendons. Focused shockwave is particularly effective for calcific tendinitis, where it can break up calcium deposits and stimulate their reabsorption by the body.
  • Patellar tendinitis (jumper's knee) — Chronic pain at the base of the kneecap, common in athletes who perform repetitive jumping, squatting, or running activities. Shockwave therapy helps promote tendon healing and reduce pain in cases that have not responded to exercise therapy alone.
  • Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis) — Chronic tendon pain at the outer elbow that fails to resolve with rest and standard physical therapy. Shockwave therapy combined with eccentric exercise is an effective non-surgical approach for persistent epicondylalgia.
  • Shin splints (medial tibial stress syndrome) — Chronic pain along the inner border of the shinbone, common in runners and athletes who increase training volume too quickly. Shockwave therapy can help reduce pain and promote healing of the periosteal irritation associated with shin splints.
  • Chronic muscle pain and trigger points — Persistent myofascial pain and trigger points that do not respond to manual therapy or dry needling alone. The acoustic waves can help release deep trigger points and reduce referred pain patterns.

What to Expect During Treatment

Understanding what happens during a shockwave therapy session can help you feel prepared and confident in the treatment process. Here is what you can expect when you come to Resolve Physical Therapy for focused shockwave therapy:

Session duration: Each shockwave therapy session lasts approximately 10 to 20 minutes of active treatment time, with your total appointment lasting around 30 minutes to allow for setup, assessment, and any complementary treatments your therapist may recommend. Your therapist will apply ultrasound gel to the treatment area and use the shockwave handpiece to deliver precisely targeted acoustic pulses to the injured tissue.

Number of sessions: Most conditions respond well to three to six sessions of focused shockwave therapy, typically spaced one to two weeks apart. Your therapist will assess your progress after each session and adjust the treatment parameters and plan as needed. Some patients experience significant improvement after just one or two sessions, while more chronic conditions may require the full course of treatment.

Sensation during treatment: You may experience some discomfort during the treatment, often described as a strong tapping or pulsing sensation at the treatment site. Your therapist will adjust the intensity to your tolerance level throughout the session, and the discomfort typically decreases as the area becomes desensitized during treatment. The treatment is well-tolerated by the vast majority of patients and does not require any anesthesia.

After treatment: You may experience mild soreness or redness in the treated area for 24 to 48 hours following a shockwave therapy session, similar to post-exercise soreness. This is a normal response and indicates that the healing process has been activated. We recommend avoiding high-impact activities on the treated area for 48 hours after each session, but you can typically return to normal daily activities immediately.

Potential Benefits of Shockwave Therapy

Patients who undergo focused shockwave therapy at Resolve Physical Therapy commonly experience the following benefits:

  • Tissue regeneration — Shockwave therapy stimulates the body's natural repair mechanisms, promoting the growth of new, healthy tissue to replace damaged or degenerated structures. This is particularly important for chronic tendon conditions where the body's natural healing process has stalled.
  • Accelerated healing — By restarting the body's healing cascade and delivering concentrated energy to the damaged tissue, shockwave therapy can significantly accelerate recovery times compared to rest and conservative management alone.
  • Improved blood flow — The formation of new blood vessels (neovascularization) increases blood supply to the treated area, delivering essential nutrients and growth factors that support tissue repair. This is especially valuable for tendon injuries, as tendons have naturally limited blood supply.
  • Pain relief — Shockwave therapy reduces pain through the depletion of pain neurotransmitters, stimulation of endorphin release, and reduction of inflammatory mediators. Many patients report significant pain reduction that persists and continues to improve in the weeks following their final treatment session.
  • Enhanced mobility — As pain decreases and tissue quality improves, patients typically experience improved range of motion, reduced stiffness, and greater ease of movement in activities that were previously painful or limited.
  • Non-invasive alternative — Shockwave therapy provides a non-surgical treatment option for chronic conditions that may otherwise require surgical intervention. It involves no incisions, no medication, and no extended recovery periods, making it an attractive option for patients who want to avoid more invasive procedures.
  • Precision depth targeting — Unlike radial shockwave devices limited to 3–4 cm, focused shockwave reaches structures up to 12 cm deep. This allows effective treatment of deep tendons, calcifications, and entheses that other shockwave devices cannot access.

Contraindications and Safety

Focused shockwave therapy is a safe treatment when performed by a trained therapist and used for appropriate conditions. However, there are certain situations in which shockwave therapy should not be used. Your therapist will conduct a thorough screening before beginning treatment to ensure your safety.

Shockwave therapy is not recommended for patients who:

  • Are pregnant or may be pregnant
  • Are currently taking blood-thinning medications (anticoagulants)
  • Have a bleeding disorder or blood clotting condition
  • Have an active blood clot (deep vein thrombosis) in the treatment area
  • Have an active infection in the treatment area
  • Have a known or suspected cancer in the treatment area
  • Have an implanted cardiac pacemaker or other electronic implant
  • Have metal implants (plates, screws, or joint replacements) directly in the treatment area
  • Require treatment over the lungs (risk of pneumothorax)
  • Are skeletally immature (treatment should not be applied over open growth plates in children and adolescents)
