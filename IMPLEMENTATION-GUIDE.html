Website Implementation Guide

Resolve Physical Therapy — Bend, Oregon

Your step-by-step guide to launching your new website
Estimated total time: 2–3 hours
No technical experience required

You have a complete, ready-to-go website built with standard web files (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript) — no fancy frameworks or databases required. To get it live on the internet, you need to do four things: make a few text edits, set up a free form service, upload everything to a hosting provider, and handle basic privacy items. Total: 2–3 hours

Roadmap — Do These Steps In This Order

  1. Step 1: Edit Your Website Files ~60–90 min
    • Edits #1–#12: Critical fixes (phone, email, photos, forms, video, maps)
    • Edits #13–#16: Polish items (favicon, privacy links, OG images)
  2. Step 2: Set Up Your Contact Forms ~15 min
    • Web3Forms (recommended), Formspree, or Netlify Forms
  3. Step 3: Choose Hosting & Go Live ~30 min
    • Netlify (easiest), Cloudflare Pages (fastest), or Namecheap (email included)
  4. Step 4: Privacy, Legal & Compliance ~15 min
    • HIPAA, privacy policy, accessibility, Google Business Profile
ⓘ Why this order? If you upload your site first and then make edits, you have to upload again. By editing first, you only upload once and your site is ready from the moment it goes live.

Before You Begin

What You Need:

  • Notepad++ installed — free download: notepad-plus-plus.org/downloads/
  • Your website folder on your computer (the folder with index.html, services.html, about.html, contact.html)
  • About 2–3 hours of focused time

How Find & Replace Works (You Will Use This a LOT):

  1. With the file open in Notepad++, press Ctrl+H on your keyboard
  2. In the Find what: box, paste exactly what the instructions say
  3. In the Replace with: box, paste exactly what the instructions say
  4. Click Replace All (or just Replace to go one at a time)
  5. Press Ctrl+S to save the file
💡 If Notepad++ says "0 replacements found" — the spacing in your file might be slightly different. Try using Ctrl+F (Find) first to locate the text, then edit it by hand. The important thing is finding the right text and changing it.

Deploy Your Site:

  1. Go to netlify.com and create a free account
  2. On your dashboard, find the dotted-line drag-and-drop area
  3. Open File Explorer, find your website folder
  4. Drag the entire folder onto the dotted-line box in your browser

  1. In Netlify → Site Settings → Domain Management → Add custom domain

  3. At your domain registrar, add a CNAME record: Host = www, Value = your Netlify URL
  4. Wait 5 min to 48 hours for DNS to update (usually under 1 hour)
  5. SSL activates automatically once the domain is connected

Privacy, Legal & Compliance

Handle basic privacy, accessibility, and Google Business items

4A: Do You Need HIPAA Compliance?

✅ Short answer: No, not for this website.
Your contact forms collect name, email, phone, and a message — none of which is Protected Health Information (PHI). You're in the clear.
⚠️ One important warning: Do NOT add form fields asking visitors to describe specific health conditions, injuries, or diagnoses. That could cross the line into collecting PHI.

If you ever want online intake forms collecting health history, use a HIPAA-compliant platform:

  • Jane App (janeapp.com) — popular with PT clinics
  • IntakeQ (intakeq.com) — specializes in online intake
  • Prompt EMR's patient portal — check if it supports this

4B: Privacy Policy

Recommended but not legally required for a small clinic site. Builds trust with visitors.

What to include:

  1. What information you collect (names, emails, phones from forms)
  2. How you use it (respond to inquiries, schedule appointments)
  3. Who you share it with (nobody except the form delivery service)
  4. Third-party services (Google Fonts — Google receives visitor IP addresses)
  5. Contact info for privacy questions

Create a privacy-policy.html page using the template provided in the full guide. Then update all footer "Privacy Policy" links from href="#" to href="privacy-policy.html".

4C: Do You Need a Cookie Banner?

✅ No, you do not. Your website uses no cookies, no analytics, no tracking scripts, and no advertising pixels. Cookie consent banners are only required when your site places cookies on visitors' devices.

If you add Google Analytics or Facebook Pixel later, you WILL need a banner at that point.

4D: ADA/WCAG Accessibility

Federal deadlines: 15+ employees by May 2026; smaller practices by May 2027.

✅ Your website was built with accessibility as a priority. Already in place: semantic HTML, skip links, form labels, ARIA attributes, keyboard navigation, high contrast text.

Rules for when you edit:

  • Always add descriptive alt text to images
  • Use headings in order (h1 → h2 → h3, never skip)
  • Use descriptive link text (not "Click here")
  • Check color contrast at webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker/

4E: Google Business Profile

  1. Go to business.google.com and sign in
  2. Find your Resolve Physical Therapy listing
  3. Click Edit profile → update the Website field to https://resolveptbend.com
  4. While there, verify: business name, address, phone, hours, categories, and photos

Updating Your Site After Future Edits

After your site is live, you'll occasionally need to make changes. Here's how to re-upload for each provider:

Netlify

  1. Log in to app.netlify.com → click your site → Deploys tab
  2. Drag your updated website folder onto the deploy area
  3. Changes are live within seconds

Cloudflare Pages

  1. Log in to dash.cloudflare.com → Workers & Pages → your project
  2. Click Create deployment → Upload assets
  3. Drag your updated folder in → click Deploy site
💡 Cloudflare keeps deployment history. You can roll back if something goes wrong.

Namecheap (cPanel)

  1. Log in → Go to cPanel → File Manager → public_html
  2. Upload only the files you changed
  3. Overwrite when prompted — changes are live immediately

General Tips for Updates

  • Always test locally first. Open your edited HTML files in your browser before uploading.
  • Keep a backup. Copy your website folder before major changes (e.g., Website Backup - Feb 2026).
  • Clear your browser cache. Press Ctrl+Shift+R if changes don't appear after re-uploading.
