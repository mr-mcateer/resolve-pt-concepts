## Services Overview

### 1. Post-Operative Care
Physical Therapy following a surgery such as operations on the hip, knee, shoulder, wrist, neck, foot, ankle, and spine to facilitate a speedy recovery.

**Three-Stage Recovery Framework:**
- Stage 1 - Immediate Post-Surgery: Initial healing with immobilization, swelling reduction, pain management
- Stage 2 - Progressive Rehabilitation: Customized exercise programs for strengthening, range of motion, stability
- Stage 3 - Return to Activity: Working toward pre-surgery activity levels

**Key Benefits:**
- Proper healing & faster recovery (personalized therapy minimizes scar tissue, reestablishes joint function)
- Regain mobility (particularly important for knee and hip surgeries)
- Patient participation leads to better outcomes

**Additional therapeutic outcomes:** Circulation improvement, blood clot prevention, posture/balance training, gait analysis, pain management, flexibility, reduced pulmonary complications

**Sub-conditions treated:**
- Total Knee Replacements
- Total Hip Replacements
- Rotator Cuff Repairs
- ACL Reconstructions
- Meniscus Repairs
- Achilles Repairs

### 2. Focused Shockwave Therapy (NEW - highlighted service)
Non-invasive treatment using high-energy sound waves to stimulate healing in chronic tendon, ligament, and soft tissue conditions.

**Conditions treated:**
- Plantar fasciitis
- Achilles tendinopathy
- Rotator cuff injuries
- Patellar tendinitis
- Tennis elbow
- Shin splints
- Chronic muscle pain trigger points

**Treatment details:**
- Duration: 10-20 minutes per session
- Typically 3-6 sessions needed
- May experience temporary discomfort during treatment

**Potential benefits:** Tissue regeneration, accelerated healing, improved blood flow, chronic pain relief, enhanced mobility

**Contraindications:** Pregnancy, blood thinners, bleeding disorders, over blood clots/infections/active cancer/pacemakers/metal implants/lungs/growth plates

**Pricing:**
- First 30-minute session: FREE
- Additional sessions: $150 per 30 minutes
- NOT covered by insurance

### 3. Running Rehab
Comprehensive running rehabilitation using advanced analysis technology.

**Running Evaluation Program: $300 (two one-hour appointments)**
Includes:
- Thorough PT evaluation
- Video gait analysis (multiple angles, slow-motion, treadmill)
- DorsaVi wearable sensor analysis (2 sensors on shins)
- Shoe evaluation
- Personalized treatment plan

**DorsaVi Sensor Measurements:**
- Ground reaction forces
- Initial peak acceleration
- Ground contact time
- Cadence
- Single limb stability (double leg squat, single leg squat, single leg hop)

**Video Gait Analysis evaluates:** Posture, arm swing, instability, balance, stride length

### 4. Injuries
Physical Therapy intervention can be used to treat a multitude of injuries and diagnoses from lower back pain, to a sports injury, to pain after a motor vehicle accident.

**Injuries are an unfortunate part of life, especially for active individuals and athletes. Physical therapy is the most effective method for combatting an injury and preventing future injury.**

Sub-categories: Lower Back Pain, Neck Pain, Motor Vehicle Accidents, Sports Injuries, Running Injuries, Epicondylalgia

### 5. Manual Therapy
A clinical approach utilizing skilled, specific hands-on techniques, including but not limited to manipulation/mobilization, used by the physical therapist to diagnose and treat soft tissues and joint structures for the purpose of modulating pain; increasing range of motion (ROM); reducing or eliminating soft tissue inflammation; inducing relaxation; improving contractile and non-contractile tissue repair, extensibility, and/or stability; facilitating movement; and improving function.

**Techniques offered:**
- Soft Tissue Massage
- IASTM (Instrument-Assisted Soft Tissue Mobilization)
- Passive Range of Motion
- Joint Mobilizations

### 6. Vestibular Rehab Therapy
An exercise-based program, designed by a specialty-trained vestibular physical therapist, to improve balance and reduce problems related to dizziness.

**Assessment includes:** Patient symptom evaluation, medical history, balance/gait assessment, neck motion, visual stability

**Goals:** Improve functional abilities, reduce fall risk, enhance quality of life

**Conditions treated:**
- Vertigo
- Post Concussion
- Balance Impairments
- Dizziness

### 7. Neuro Rehab
Neurological Physical Therapy focuses on restoring function and improving quality of life in patients with varying types of neurological conditions.

### 8. Chronic Pain
Pain that lasts more than 3 months is considered to be chronic in nature. The brain says there is a threat to your body based on the input that it receives from your nervous system -- sometimes without actual tissue damage.

**Conditions treated:** Trauma/injury, Diabetes, Complex Regional Pain Syndrome, Fibromyalgia, Cancer, Arthritis

**Treatment approach:**
- Patient education on pain neuroscience
- Sleep, stress, and nutrition guidance
- Self-empowerment strategies
- Activity modification
- Low-impact aerobic exercise
- Therapeutic stretching and strengthening
- Manual therapy

## Service Detail Pages

### Lower Back Pain
More people struggle with back pain than any other orthopedic condition.

**Treatment approaches:**
- Stretching: Exercise and proper stretching of muscles in the lower back, abdomen, hips, and legs is imperative
- Movement Control Exercises: Retrains spinal muscles to support the spine
- Manual Therapy: Joint and soft tissue mobilization, dry needling, passive stretching, myofascial release
- Patient Education: Teaching patients to understand and manage their pain

### Neck Pain
**Common causes:** Desk work without changing positions, sleeping in bad position, repetitive movements, poor posture
**Medical conditions:** Osteoarthritis, Osteoporosis, Rheumatoid Arthritis, Herniated Disks, Fibromyalgia, Meningitis (rare)
**Prevention:** Frequent breaks, proper posture, ergonomic positioning, headsets, avoid heavy shoulder bags, quit smoking

### Sports Injuries Treated
- Patellofemoral Syndrome
- Concussion
- ACL Tear
- Hip Flexor Strain
- Shin Splints
- Sciatica
- Shoulder Injuries (dislocations, misalignments, strains, sprains)
- Tennis/Golf Elbow
- Groin Pull
- Hamstring Strain

### Running Injuries
65% of all runners experience a running injury in a given year. Typically from repetitive overuse.

**Common running injuries:** Calf strains, Plantar Fasciitis, Hip Bursitis, IT Band Friction Syndrome, Patellofemoral Pain, Shin Splints, Hamstring Strains, Tibialis Posterior Tendonitis

**Evaluation includes:** Medical history, symptoms, strength/endurance testing, flexibility, foot/knee/hip mechanics, gait analysis, footwear assessment

### Epicondylalgia (Tennis Elbow)
Pain and decreased motion of the elbow and arm due to swelling of the tendons on the outside portion of the forearm. Physical therapy reduces swelling/inflammation and restores function.

### ACL Reconstructions
ACL found diagonally in the middle of the knee, prevents shinbone from sliding forward. Injuries during sports with sudden stops, jumping, direction changes. Most torn ACLs require surgical replacement with tissue grafts. Recovery: 6+ months before return to sports. Rehab: restore motion, progressive strengthening, sport-specific training.

### Total Knee Replacement
Resurfacing damaged knee using metal and plastic parts. PT begins within 3 days of discharge. First 2 weeks: manage pain, decrease swelling, heal incision, restore walking, initiate exercise. Program includes ROM exercises, strengthening, soft tissue massage with scar mobilization, balance training, functional activities.

### Total Hip Replacement
Surgical replacement with artificial prostheses. Surgeon removes diseased bone, implants prosthetic socket and femoral head. Most common cause: osteoarthritis. Critical precaution: avoid crossing legs or bending hip beyond 90 degrees post-surgery.
