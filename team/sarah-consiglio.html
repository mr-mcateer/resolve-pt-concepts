About Sarah

Sarah Consiglio brings a uniquely multidisciplinary perspective to her practice at Resolve Physical Therapy in Bend, Oregon. Her academic journey began at Southern Oregon University, where she earned her Bachelor of Science in Psychology. That foundation in understanding human behavior, cognition, and the mind-body connection has proven to be an invaluable asset in her work as a physical therapist. Sarah then completed her Doctorate of Physical Therapy at Oregon State University - Cascades, the Bend-based campus that allowed her to train in the very community she now serves. This local education gave her hands-on clinical experience in Central Oregon's healthcare landscape, treating the conditions and patient populations that walk through the door at Resolve every day.

Sarah's career path, from psychology to physical therapy, reflects a deep commitment to understanding people as whole individuals, not just collections of symptoms. She recognizes that a patient's emotional state, stress levels, personal history, and psychological relationship with pain all influence their physical recovery. This holistic awareness allows her to build treatment plans that address not just the mechanical components of an injury but also the motivational, emotional, and behavioral factors that determine whether a patient truly gets better and stays better.

Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome and Hypermobility

Sarah has developed a particular passion and expertise in treating patients with Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome and hypermobility spectrum disorders. These connective tissue conditions present some of the most complex challenges in physical therapy. Patients with EDS often have joints that move beyond normal ranges, tissues that are slower to heal, and pain patterns that do not follow conventional orthopedic rules. Many EDS patients have been dismissed or misunderstood by healthcare providers who are not familiar with the condition, and they arrive at Resolve having already endured years of frustration and inadequate care.

Sarah approaches EDS treatment with the patience, knowledge, and sensitivity these patients deserve. She understands that aggressive stretching and high-impact exercise programs, which might be appropriate for other orthopedic patients, can actually make hypermobility conditions worse. Instead, she designs gentle, targeted rehabilitation programs focused on joint stability, proprioceptive awareness, and controlled strengthening. The goal is not to push joints to their end ranges but to build the muscular support system that helps hypermobile joints function safely and with less pain during everyday activities and the recreational pursuits her patients love.

Her work with EDS patients extends to education and self-management strategies. Sarah teaches patients how to recognize the early warning signs of a joint flare-up, how to modify activities to reduce subluxation risk, and how to build sustainable exercise habits that maintain stability without triggering a symptom cycle. This empowerment-focused approach gives EDS patients something many have never had: a clear, evidence-based plan for managing their condition long term.

Pediatric Orthopedics

Sarah's clinical training also includes significant experience in pediatric orthopedics. Treating children and adolescents requires a different therapeutic skill set than working with adults. Young patients are still developing both physically and emotionally, and their conditions must be assessed within the context of growth plates, developmental milestones, and age-appropriate activity demands. Sarah brings a warm, creative approach to pediatric care that puts young patients at ease while ensuring their treatment is grounded in evidence-based practice. She works closely with parents and families to explain diagnoses, set realistic expectations, and create home exercise programs that children can actually follow and sustain.

Adaptive Sports and Inclusive Rehabilitation

Another area where Sarah excels is adaptive sports rehabilitation. She is passionate about ensuring that physical therapy is accessible and effective for individuals of all abilities. Whether working with an athlete who uses adaptive equipment, a patient recovering from a condition that has changed their physical capabilities, or someone navigating a new diagnosis that affects their mobility, Sarah tailors her approach to meet each person exactly where they are. Her philosophy is straightforward: every person deserves the opportunity to be active and to participate in the activities that bring them joy, and it is her job to help find the path that makes that possible.

Post-Operative Care

Sarah also brings strong skills in post-operative rehabilitation, working with patients recovering from a range of surgical procedures including joint replacements, ligament reconstructions, and spinal surgeries. Her meticulous attention to surgical protocols, combined with her ability to read a patient's pain response and adjust treatment accordingly, produces strong outcomes and high patient satisfaction. The one-on-one treatment model at Resolve is especially important in post-surgical care, where close monitoring and individualized progression are critical to a safe and successful recovery.

Outside the Clinic

She lives with her husband and their dog, and together they take full advantage of the outdoor recreation that makes Bend such a special place to call home. Hiking and camping in the Cascade Range are favorite pastimes, along with paddleboarding on the area's many lakes and rivers when the weather cooperates. Sarah's love for the outdoors goes beyond exercise; it is a source of inspiration and balance that she carries into her work every day.

Beyond the trails and waterways, Sarah has a creative side that she nurtures through crafting, cooking, and baking. She enjoys experimenting with new recipes, working on craft projects, and settling in with a good book after a long day. These interests reflect the same patience, attention to detail, and creativity that define her approach to physical therapy: a willingness to try new things, an eye for the details that matter, and a genuine enjoyment of the process of building something meaningful.
